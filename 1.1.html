Key Considerations for Your Retirement Planning

  • The power of **compounding** is evident in the 'Investment Growth' portion of your projected balance. The earlier you start, the more significant its impact.
  • **Inflation** significantly reduces future purchasing power. Regularly review and adjust your contribution goals to outpace inflation.
  • Your **expected annual return** is a critical variable. While 7% is a common average for diversified investments, market performance can vary. Consider building models with lower returns for conservative planning.
  • Even small **increases in annual contributions** can lead to substantially higher balances over many years. Experiment with increasing your 'Annual Contribution' to see the difference.
  • Always max out your **employer match** if possible – it's free money for your retirement and an immediate, guaranteed return on your contributions.
  • This calculation assumes consistent contributions and returns. Actual market performance fluctuates, and life events can alter plans. Regularly **review and adjust** your strategy with a financial advisor.

Important Assumptions:

  • **Data Privacy:** This calculator operates entirely client-side. **No user inputs or calculated results are ever sent to or stored on our servers.** All data processing occurs locally within your web browser.
  • Contributions and employer match are assumed to occur consistently at the **beginning of each compounding period**.
  • The 'Expected Annual Return' is a **pre-tax average** and does not account for specific investment fees not represented by the net return.
  • 'Inflation Rate' is applied uniformly over the retirement period to adjust purchasing power.
  • This tool **does not account for income taxes on capital gains or during investment (except for explicit withdrawal taxation)**, specific 401K plan administration fees (beyond what's reflected in net return), or potential required minimum distributions (RMDs) during retirement.
  • The 'Estimated After-Tax Balance' for **Traditional 401K** is a simplification applying your 'Expected Retirement Tax Rate' directly to the nominal balance. For **Roth 401K**, it's assumed tax-free.
